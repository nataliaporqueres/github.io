Natalia Porqueres

Cosmologist at CEA Saclay

I am a permanent researcher in the Cosmostat group at CEA Paris-Saclay, where I develop new methods to analyse data from cosmological surveys. My research aims to make an optimal use of weak lensing observations to investigate dark energy, dark matter, and the formation of cosmic structures. I am the principal investigator of the OCAPi project (starting in 2026), funded by an ERC Starting Grant, which analyses weak lensing maps at the pixel level. This novel approach improves the precision of cosmological parameter constraints and provides a map of the matter distribution in the Universe, effectively making the dark matter visible.

Based at CEA Saclay.
